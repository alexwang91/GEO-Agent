# Personas

These personas define the first technical-preview users for the AI Search Visibility Experiment Workbench. They constrain UI language, report sections, task ownership, and error copy.

## Growth lead

Goal: understand whether priority products, features, or categories appear in AI answers.

Needs: directional visibility, missing-query evidence, competitor context, task owners, and retest timing.

Copy rule: low-sample results are directional until confidence and noise-floor checks exist.

## SEO and content strategist

Goal: find owned-page gaps that prevent a brand from being cited or accurately described.

Needs: cited-source map, page snapshots, claim support, topic gaps, content edits, and retest clusters.

Copy rule: recommendations must stay evidence-backed and query-specific.

## PR and communications owner

Goal: identify third-party sources, reviews, directories, community pages, and earned media that influence AI answers.

Needs: source classification, third-party evidence gaps, citation quality, and safe outreach tasks.

Copy rule: separate observed evidence from inferred cause.

## Engineering and data owner

Goal: keep audit packages reproducible, redacted, and traceable.

Needs: package manifest, source IDs, sample IDs, provider status, and failure details.

Copy rule: error copy must preserve actionable detail without exposing credentials or raw access values.

## Persona language rules

- Use **manual** for user-supplied or recorded evidence.
- Use **simulated** for fixture-backed or fake-provider evidence used by deterministic tests.
- Use **live configured** only when an implemented provider is explicitly configured by the user.
- Use **planned** for unavailable roadmap providers.
- Use **directional** for low-sample metrics.
